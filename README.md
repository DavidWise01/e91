E91 &mdash; David Lee Wise (ROOT0). Ekert&#39;s 1991 entanglement-based QKD. Alice and Bob each hold one half of a stream of entangled pairs and measure along randomly chosen axes. The runs where they happened to pick the SAME axis are perfectly (anti)correlated and become the key; the OTHER runs are spent on a BELL / CHSH test. If the correlations VIOLATE the Bell inequality (CHSH &gt; 2, up to the quantum bound 2&radic;2 &asymp; 2.828), then no local-hidden-variable eavesdropper could have been in the line &mdash; the security is CERTIFIED by the physics, not assumed. &#9670; LIT: the entangled correlations, the same-axis sift and the live CHSH value are computed as you add noise / eavesdropping and watch the Bell value fall toward the classical bound of 2.
